The Transformer architecture replaces recurrence with self-attention. Each token
attends to every other token in the sequence, so the model can capture long-range
dependencies in a single layer. Multi-head attention lets the model attend to
information from different representation subspaces at once.

Because attention compares every pair of tokens, its cost grows quadratically
with sequence length. This is why long documents are split into smaller chunks
before they are embedded, and why chunk size is bounded by the embedding model's
maximum context length.
